About The Cook

Hi! I’m Graciella, and welcome to my home, Bahay Kubo. This is where you’ll find traditional Filipino recipes and cooking tips that celebrate the essence of our culture. Ever since I was young, I’ve been fascinated by Filipino food. It’s more than just meals — these dishes are a celebration of our culture, traditions, and the times when families come together. That’s something I hold close to my heart, and I want to make sure we pass that on to future generations without losing the soul of Filipino cuisine.

If you’ve ever tried searching for authentic Filipino recipes online, you might have had the same struggle I did. Many resources are hard to find, hidden behind paywalls, or simply don’t capture the true flavors of our food. That’s why I created Bahay Kubo — my way of sharing the rich and diverse flavors of Filipino cuisine with anyone who’s interested, whether you’re a local Filipino or someone from abroad who’s eager to experience this part of our culture.

Here, you’ll find recipes that are easy to follow but remain true to our heritage. As someone from Pampanga, the culinary capital of the Philippines, I’ve always been surrounded by amazing food, and I’m passionate about keeping that tradition alive. Cooking has always been a passion of mine, but like many of us, I didn’t always have the right resources to get started. Now, I’m excited to provide this platform where you can explore and try out recipes that bring the warmth of Filipino home-cooked meals to your kitchen. Whether you’re just starting out or already a seasoned cook, I hope Bahay Kubo feels like home to you. So grab your ingredients, explore the recipes, and welcome to my Bahay Kubo!

Frequently Asked Questions

I have a question about the recipe. Can you help?

Absolutely, I’m here to help! It’s completely normal to feel a bit intimidated when trying a new cuisine, but Filipino cooking can be simple and fun. Start with the easy recipes, follow the steps closely, and don’t hesitate to ask questions if something isn’t clear. Cooking is all about practice, so don’t worry about making mistakes—it’s part of the learning process! To stay updated on all my recipes, you can subscribe to my newsletter or follow me on social media. I regularly share new recipes, tips, and cooking tricks to make your Filipino cooking journey enjoyable.

Are the recipes on your website beginner-friendly?

Yes! All my recipes are designed to be beginner-friendly. I provide step-by-step instructions and tips to help you succeed in the kitchen, even if you’re new to cooking.

What ingredients do I need to start cooking Filipino food?

Common ingredients in Filipino cooking include rice, soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, onion, ginger, and a variety of vegetables and proteins. I recommend starting with a few essential items and gradually adding more as you try different recipes.

Can I substitute ingredients in the recipes?

Yes! Feel free to substitute ingredients based on your preferences or what you have available. For example, if a recipe calls for pork and you prefer chicken, you can make that swap. Just keep in mind that some substitutions might slightly change the flavor or texture.

How can I store leftover food?

Leftovers can be stored in airtight containers in the refrigerator for up to 3-4 days. You can also freeze dishes for longer storage, but be sure to check specific recipes for freezing guidelines, as some dishes freeze better than others.

Do you have any tips for meal prepping Filipino dishes?

Meal prepping is a great idea! Cook larger portions of your favorite dishes, divide them into meal-sized containers, and store them in the fridge or freezer. You can also prepare ingredients in advance, like chopping vegetables or marinating meats, to save time during the week.

How often do you update the recipes on your website?

I aim to update the website regularly with new recipes, cooking tips, and techniques. Make sure to subscribe to my newsletter or follow me on social media to catch all the updates!
